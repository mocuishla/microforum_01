Part Four

Seamus dejó caer al Leprechaun
y corrió a casa tan rápido como pudo
para traer una pala.
Tras cinco minutos, estaba de vuelta al campo.
"¡Pero qué ven mis ojos!"
cada planta de trigo del campo
--y había un centenar de ellas--
tenía atada un pañuelo rojo.

Así que el joven Seamus caminó a casa con su pala.
No obtuvo su oro.
No obtuvo al Leprechaun.
E incluso no recuperó su panñuelo.

Seamus dropped the leprechaun
and ran home as fast as he could to fetch a shovel.
After five minutes, he was back at the field.
"But what a sight met his eyes!"
Every single wheat plant in the field
-- and there were hundreds of them --
had a red neckerchief tied around it.

Slowly, young Seamus walked home with his shovel.
He didn't have his gold.
He didn't have the leprechaun.
And now, he didn't even recover his neckerchief.
